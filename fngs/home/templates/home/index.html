Welcome!

Unlock the mysteries of the brain, one connectome at a time!

The brain, while superficially elegant, is an ovewhelmingly complex device consisting of billions of microscopic cells firing in unique patterns to create what we observe as thought. Imaging techniques such as functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI) allow us to see these activation patterns in real time at a fine spatial resolution. These images contain highly valuable information that can be leveraged to further our understanding of the brain.

The problem

Processing these graphs is a complex and expensive procedure. The initial investment in a processing pipeline may require hundreds of hours of labor to establish, and even using existing open source cutting edge tools requires a background in computer science and computer vision just to get the software installed and functioning properly; coupled with the extensive sets of options for processing, researchers looking to enter the computational neuroscience field may simply be barred by the learning curve to get involved.

What is FNGS?

The Functional Neurodata Graphs Service is a one-click pipeline that boasts a full suite of fMRI processing methods. It leverages cutting-edge open-source software with many novel algorithms, all combined in a simple-to-deploy docker container that functions on the cloud. The processing steps include template registration, nuisance correction, timeseries extraction, and much more. The pipeline outputs highly relevant connectome graphs along with quality assurance figures every step of the way.

Motivation

Through FNGS, we hope to lower the barrier to entry of computational neuroscience. Our user-friendly UI coupled with the integration of AWS cloud services into the pipeline enables researchers to process their brain scans with lightning fast efficiency on the click of a button. Our research into high-quality fMRI processing algorithms ensures that users will be able to trust the easy-to-understand qualitative and quantitative outputs they receive.
